BORN A CRIME

by Trevor Noah

Blog 1: Marriage

Why do women suffer the most in this world? I remember reading somewhere in the book that Dinky, the husband to Trevor's aunt pretended to control her because he believed that a husband should be dominant and controlling. That is not the case since everyone is equal and is free to express themselves. Trevor also remembers being told that: If you don't hit your woman, you don't love herLike Is that really the case? Anyway, women are not taken are not respected in the world we are living in. That needs to be put to an end. Up to now, I still do not understand why people believed that women should only submit and obey. Women deserve a lot of respect in the world because they are the most amazing and kind people yet no one sees this. Patricia marries Abel not because she needed him but because she likes him. Patricia and Trevor are radically independent and do not need anyone to round our their family. Abel shows his true intentions of controlling Patricia. He is bound to the Tsonga traditional belief that a man should control his wife and treat her like a servant. That is what most cultures believe to date, my culture being among them. I don't think that matters since every gender is capable of providing for the family. What I liked about Patricia is that she is an independent woman and she stands for the truth. Abel begins acting out of the standard gender roles of Tsonga culture, based on a fundamental that he owns his family and thus gets to decide what he wants to do with them. Abel becomes more violent especially when he is drunk.

He would use force to control Patricia's actions. Patricia has no interest in giving him a second chance or the control that he is craving for. In fact, she stands her ground on principle and, he attacks her because he is incapable of resolving the conflicts. He is unable to justify why she owes him complete control over her life. Abel resorts to brute force, which shows he is a coward man. I think if Patricia got a husband that was ready to sit down with her to settle conflicts, they would have gone far. Patricia is fed up with all the brutal beatings from Abel and therefore goes to the police station. The South African Police have little interest in protecting everyone equally. When there is obvious proof of Abel's assault, they do nothing and to my shock, they are even taking his side and treating him as the victim of his wife audacious enough to report abuse. This shows how the world disrespect and discriminates against women. Patricia invests in Abel, tries to save his business for him, and perfectly capable of doing so, while he views her as a piece of property to be owned and controlled. I think Abel would have been grateful and consider himself as one of the luckiest men for having such a loving and caring wife. It is high time for the cultures to change the belief of seeing a woman as useless in the community. Patricia is helpful since she helps in investing in Abel's business. She trusts Abel and this draws the family back to poverty. They lose their Eden Park home to the black tax. Patricia does everything to put things in place in place but the primitive man, Abel with his fixed mind does not see the need. We are told Patricia takes on the traditional male role instead of the female one. I think the decision she makes is for the betterment of her family. The family would starve if she listens to Abel because Abel did not do his responsibilities well since he was always not in his right mind. What amazes me is what Abel ever wants is to control Patricia by using force not thinking that Patricia would leave him. He even goes to the extent of buying a gun to scare everyone not knowing that these people would not want to associate with him. Patricia insists on going to the police but all goes in vain. Trevor realizes that his mother is serious about Abel's threats and focused more on her safety than her independence. This shows how clever Trevor's mother was. She was even optimistic after Abel shot her. Women should be considered as the most powerful people in th world. They goes through a lot of difficulties but still acts normal.

Blog 2: Humour

Born a crime is one of the funniest books I have ever read in my whole life. The book is not just funny but, it enlarges our understanding of the world. Noah offers an understanding of South African history and colonialism. He used humor to explain most instances of his life. Some of which were sad but Trevor explained them in a funny way. One incident is seen at the beginning of the book where he said that his mother threw him out of a moving car when a driver wanted to kill them. He blamed his mother for throwing him out before waking up. At least they would have woken me up before they killed me. We see after the incident Trevor and his mum are able to laugh despite of being in so much pain. There's a humor where Trevor's mother is shot."My mother looks at me and goes, 'shhh, don't cry baby.''I say, No, mom, I'm gonna cry, you were shot in the head!'No, no no, look on the bright side.''What bright side?'' At least now in my nose, you are officially the best-looking person in the family.'Humor helped Noah's family heal from the shock they were in since Abel had shot her thrice in the head.Even there was nothing to joke about Noah's mother still made a joke through it. And this helped the family not to worry too much. There's also a humor when Trevor's mom befriends a woman who is of his color. And this woman acts like Trevor's mom. When they are waliking in the streets Yrevor's mom would waliking walk away from them and act like she was his maid. This really helped her from losing her child, Trevor because if she risked walking with him he would have been taken to an orphanage if he was discovered as from a mixed-race family. Trevor's father would have paid a fine and his mother arrested.

Trevor also joke about his birth. He says his parents are legally forbidden to marry, broke the law not only for being together but also by having him. He calls his memoir, 'Born a crime' I remembered my dad used to love running with me. I used to remember running with him in the Park and running with him in the street. And then when I got older, and I realized about the laws of South Africa , he wasn't running with me, he was running from me. This was a man who couldn't be seen with this child, because then the game would be up, you know? The lie would be revealed. I can say Trevor is optimistic about every situations in his life including the difficult times.
